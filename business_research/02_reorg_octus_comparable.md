# Comparable — Reorg / Octus (data collected from research subagent)

This is the closest big-comparable to what Aetherius pretends to be at scale. Useful as an anchor for pricing and buyer archetype math, NOT as a business Aetherius should try to become in year 1.

## Customer counts (all disclosed by the company at different points)

- 2016: "More than 350 subscribers pay from $50,000 to $150,000 a year" — Forbes, Daniel Fisher, Jun 1 2016 (founder Kent Collier disclosure). [Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/danielfisher/2016/06/01/in-or-out-of-bankrtuptcy-reorg-is-watching/)
- 2022: "More than 25,000 professionals across the world's leading financial institutions, law firms and professional services businesses" — Permira/Businesswire acquisition release, Aug 1 2022. [Businesswire](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20220731005068/en/Permira-Announces-Strategic-Investment-in-Reorg)
- 2024: "Over 35,000 industry professionals" at time of Oct 29 2024 rebrand to Octus. [Businesswire](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20241029189151/en/Reorg-Rebrands-to-Octus)
- 2025: "More than 40,000 credit professionals" — current octus.com language (conflicts with 35k figure Reuters uses in 2025).

## Institutional penetration at rebrand (2024)

100% of top-10 Bulge Bracket Banks. 100% of top-10 asset managers and CLO managers. 88% of AM Law 50 firms. All five "magic circle" London firms. [Businesswire](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20241029189151/en/Reorg-Rebrands-to-Octus).

## Pricing

- Only publicly disclosed per-organization price: **$50k–$150k per year**, Forbes 2016 (per org, not per seat, ~10 years old).
- No pricing on reorg.com or octus.com — everything gated behind "request access."
- User Agreement (Dec 2022) references a "Subscription Fee" set on the individual Subscription Form but no dollar amount.

## Valuation history

- 2018 Warburg Pincus controlling stake at ~$400M valuation
- Aug 2022 Permira acquisition at ~$1.3B valuation
- 2025 Permira preparing sale "considerably north of $4B" for 2026 auction — Reuters exclusive.
- Asymmetrix model assumes ~$250M annual revenue and 25% growth for a 12.8x forward EV/rev multiple at $4B.

## Employees (2024)

- 650+ employees globally
- 300+ credit, legal and data analysts

## Read-through for Aetherius

- The "sell credit/legal intelligence to law firms + credit funds" market is real and has a $4B outcome at the top. Aetherius will not enter this market at the top; it must enter it at the bottom, where Reorg is not competing.
- Reorg started as one analyst (Kent Collier ex-Fried Frank) writing a paid daily bankruptcy newsletter to a few desks. That is the concrete precedent for the bootstrapped path — a paid daily brief, then subscriber-count growth, then platform.
- $50k–$150k per organization for a decade-old baseline product implies there is real room at $500–$2,500 per single-target report if the buyer is smaller (family office, litigation-finance shop, small credit fund) or the ask is narrower.
