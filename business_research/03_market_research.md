# Market research — bootstrapped monetization paths for Aetherius

Compiled 2026-07-11 from 6 targeted web searches. All figures below are cited to real sources listed at the end.

## Bottom line (3 sentences)

1. **The stated "sell $2.5–10k per-target Stress-Test Decks to PE / distressed / family office / activist / litigation-finance" plan is directionally right on price but wrong on buyer for a cold, no-rolodex, non-US founder** — PE and distressed shops treat unknown vendors as Tier-1 risk and put them through 45–60 day TPRM onboarding, which breaks the 6-week first-dollar plan.
2. **The two realistic bootstrapped shapes are (a) a paid tiered newsletter with a "custom asks" upsell (Doomberg / PETITION / Grant's model), and (b) narrow-scope one-off memos to small credit funds, family offices, and search funds — where the buyer is one decision-maker with a P-Card, not a procurement team.**
3. **First dollar realistically comes from small-check buyers (search funds, litigation-finance analysts, family-office CIOs, single-name activists) at $500–$3,000 per memo, with a Substack-style credibility asset running in parallel — not from PE MDs.**

## Section 1 — Ranked buyer segments (most → least realistic for solo cold outbound, no rolodex, non-US)

| Rank | Buyer | Why realistic (or not) |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Search funds / independent sponsors doing pre-LOI on public comps** | One decision-maker, no procurement, they already pay $25–75k for boutique focused-scope CDD ([userintuition.ai](https://www.userintuition.ai/posts/commercial-due-diligence-cost/)). A $2.5k "single-target downside memo" is a rounding error and buys a real answer. |
| 2 | **Small/mid family offices ($100M–$1B AUM)** | Single CIO decision. Outsourced-research retainers already exist at **$2k–$15k/month for part-time, $15k–$50k/month for near-FTE** ([schmidtconsulting.group](https://www.schmidtconsulting.group/blog/cost-for-a-retainer/), [futuristsspeakers.com](https://www.futuristsspeakers.com/expert-network-consulting-rates-consultant/)). Cash-Card culture, low procurement drag. |
| 3 | **Litigation finance analysts / distressed shops with < $500M AUM** | They already pay for Reorg/Octus ($50k–$150k/org per year, [Forbes 2016](https://www.forbes.com/sites/danielfisher/2016/06/01/in-or-out-of-bankrtuptcy-reorg-is-watching/)); a $1–3k targeted memo on a specific credit is affordable and often bought on a personal credit card. |
| 4 | **Single-name activist funds / concentrated PMs with < 10 positions** | Real buyer of decision-critical memos, but only ~50–100 addressable shops globally and they mostly do their own work in-house. |
| 5 | **Mid-market PE associates doing pre-LOI** | Nominally in scope but hit by two killers: (a) 45–60 day TPRM onboarding for unknown vendors ([kpmg.com](https://kpmg.com/xx/en/our-insights/risk-and-regulation/future-forward-third-party-risk-management-with-managed-services.html)), (b) 6–12 week branded CDD is the incumbent workflow ($100k–$500k, [userintuition.ai](https://www.userintuition.ai/posts/commercial-due-diligence-cost/)). No unknown solo wins here without a warm intro. |
| 6 | **Bulge-bracket credit desks / large PE funds** | Already 100%-covered by Reorg/Octus ([Businesswire](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20241029189151/en/Reorg-Rebrands-to-Octus)). Not addressable cold. |

**Read-through:** the pivot memo's buyer list is not wrong, but ranks are inverted. The bottom of segments 1–3 is where a cold non-US founder can actually close in 60–90 days.

## Section 2 — Comparable bootstrapped businesses

### Independent short sellers (Muddy Waters, Kerrisdale, Culper)
Do **not** sell research. They run their own capital and publish free reports as a catalyst for their own short positions ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muddy_Waters_Research), [Institutional Investor](https://www.institutionalinvestor.com/article/how-short-sellers-are-surviving-crazy-bull-market-while-waiting-it-end)). Not a replicable revenue path unless the founder wants to become an RIA/hedge fund. **Discard as a business model.**

### Paid research newsletters (real bootstrapped precedent)
- **Doomberg** ($15/mo standard, $1,200/yr "Pro" tier): ~10,000 paid subs, ~20% on Pro = ~**$4.8M+ annual revenue** for a small team ([amediaoperator.com](https://www.amediaoperator.com/analysis/substacks-founding-members-tiers-seems-promising-does-it-work/)). Origin story: previously an energy consultancy, wrote free for 12 months, then paywalled. Grew via **Twitter/X + 100 podcast appearances/year**.
- **PETITION** ($49/mo, daily bankruptcy/restructuring newsletter): pricing power justified by frequency and unreplicable content ([readless.app](https://www.readless.app/blog/best-paid-substack-newsletters-2026)).
- **Grant's Interest Rate Observer** (~$1,175/yr institutional, off-Substack since 1983): the archetype for a solo analyst becoming an institution.
- **Substack economics:** platform + Stripe fees leave writers ~86¢ on the $. 52 newsletters earn > $500k/yr. Top 27 make ~$22M combined ([Sacra](https://sacra.com/c/substack/)).

### Boutique commercial DD firms
- Solo/boutique CDD pre-LOI clusters at **$25k–$75k for 2–4 week focused scope**, scaling to $75k–$150k ([userintuition.ai](https://www.userintuition.ai/posts/commercial-due-diligence-cost/)).
- Solo consultant hourly bands: **$300–$800/hr typical**, $1,000–$1,500 for ex-partners.
- **Aetherius pricing is 10–30× below this floor.** That is either a huge opportunity (undercut) or a signal the deliverable isn't perceived as CDD-equivalent (more likely).

### Alt-data one-off sales
- Market: **$2.8B in 2025** growing ~27% YoY ([kadoa.com/blog/alternative-data-for-hedge-funds](https://www.kadoa.com/blog/alternative-data-for-hedge-funds), [extractalpha.com](https://extractalpha.com/2024/05/23/alternative-data-for-hedge-funds/)).
- Hedge fund spend benchmark: **$1M per $1B AUM in year 1** (Morgan Stanley).
- Average dataset used by only **20 firms** (down from 25) — exclusivity is a pricing lever.
- Satellite feed: $50k–$500k/yr. Point-in-time backtestable datasets command a premium.

### Expert network calls (adjacent buyer behavior)
- Finance/PE/HF hourly: junior $100–$250, mid $250–$450, senior $400–$700, PMs $800–$1,500 ([silverlightresearch.com](https://www.silverlightresearch.com/blog/how-much-does-an-expert-interview-cost)).
- Confirms that a 2-hour written memo from a novel research shop **priced at $500–$1,500 is inside buyer habit**.

## Section 3 — Should this be a per-deck consultancy at all? Alternatives stress-tested

| Alternative | Precedent | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| **Paid Substack tiered ($15/mo standard + $1,200/yr "Pro" custom-asks upsell)** | Doomberg, PETITION, Grant's | **Strongest bootstrapped shape.** Requires 6–12 months of free publishing to build audience, but the "Pro" tier lets you sell $1,200/yr research with $0 procurement drag. |
| **Narrow-scope $500–$3k memo to search funds + family offices** | Solo CDD shops, expert network calls | **Second-strongest.** Faster first dollar (weeks not months) but poor compounding — every deliverable is bespoke labor. |
| **White-label API pull to one alt-data reseller (Neudata, Eagle Alpha, BattleFin)** | Kadoa, Similarweb re-sellers | Real market but each vendor gate takes 3–6 months. Not weeks-to-first-dollar. |
| **Risk letter to CFO/audit committee (D&O liability angle)** | No solo bootstrap precedent found | Interesting angle but corporates buy through procurement — same 45–60 day gate. Skip in year 1. |
| **Counterparty risk memo for asset-based lenders and mid-market lenders** | Boutique credit shops, factoring firms | Real buyer, small check ($500–$2k), fast decisions. **Underrated adjacent path.** |
| **Sell the backtest fixture + methodology as a conference paper / arXiv preprint** | Data science / quant researcher path | Reputation asset, not revenue. Use to unlock the newsletter/consultancy funnel. |
| **RIA-registered daily-brief subscription** | Grant's, Sevens Report | Regulatory cost + form ADV filing kill this for year 1. Reconsider at $250k ARR. |

## Section 4 — Realistic 90-day and 12-month revenue

Cold outbound benchmarks used (all from [Belkins/Instantly/Sopro data](https://belkins.io/blog/linkedin-outreach-study), 2024–25):
- LinkedIn connection acceptance: 25–30% (below 25% throttles the account)
- LinkedIn reply with personalized message: **~9.4%**
- Cold email reply financial services average: **3–5%**
- **Cold lead-to-customer close rate: ~1.7%**
- Financial-services top-quartile reply w/ good hook: ~9.3%

Applying these to a solo doing 20 personalized touches/day, 5 days/week = **100 touches/wk = 5,200/yr**:

- Replies at 5%: **260/yr**
- Qualified conversations at 30% of replies: **78/yr**
- Closed engagements at 1.7% cold benchmark of touches: **~88/yr** (upper bound, generous)
- Sane planning number: **20–40 paid engagements/yr** in year 1

### Per-deck consultancy path — 90-day floor / ceiling
- **Floor:** $0. Realistic mode for months 1–2 with no rolodex. First deck at $500–$1,500 as a paid pilot in month 2–3.
- **Ceiling (aggressive):** 2–3 paid decks at $1,500–$2,500 by day 90 = **$3–8k gross**.
- Pivot memo's "$10–25k by month 3" number assumes better hit rate than benchmarks support. **Revise down to $3–8k.**

### 12-month realistic band
| Path | Honest floor (yr 1) | Honest ceiling (yr 1) |
|---|---|---|
| **Per-deck only** | ~$15k (10 decks × $1,500) | ~$80k (30 decks × $2,500 + 1 small retainer) |
| **Substack tiered only** | $0–$2k (still audience-building) | $8–20k (first 100 paid subs at $15/mo) |
| **Both, sequenced** | $10–20k combined | $60–120k (year 2 is where compounding starts) |
| Pivot memo's stated | $60k–$400k | Overstated by ~2–3× for cold, non-US, no rolodex |

## Section 5 — Top 3 failure modes and hedges

**Failure mode 1: PE procurement wall.** Cold outreach to PE MDs will get 45–60 day TPRM queues even if reply rates are high ([kpmg TPRM](https://kpmg.com/xx/en/our-insights/risk-and-regulation/future-forward-third-party-risk-management-with-managed-services.html), [safe.security](https://safe.security/resources/blog/how-to-tier-vendors-for-effective-third-party-risk-management-tprm/)).
→ **Hedge:** target only buyer segments that can pay by P-Card / personal card in < 5 days: search funds, small family offices, litigation-finance analysts, single-name activists, ABL lenders.

**Failure mode 2: Second-target margin collapse.** SVB fixture is hand-curated; production ingestion is EDGAR-only. Second real client will require days of manual assembly — margin collapses to ~$30/hr.
→ **Hedge:** ship a real primary-news adapter and a scoped "one primary news source + EDGAR + one ratings source" pipeline before the second paid deck.

**Failure mode 3: Credibility gap for a technical audience.** Any quant reading the Apache-2.0 repo finds `core/causal_brain.py` hardcoded Discord/AWS graph and a 10-row backtest fixture — and disqualifies.
→ **Hedge:** delete or move `core/`, `orchestrator.py`, `sensory_core.py` to `archive/`. Rewrite the SVB backtest against a real archive of Reuters/Bloomberg headlines (RIT news archive, Common Crawl, or GDELT). Add 2–3 more events (Wirecard 2020, FTX 2022, First Republic 2023). Then publish it as a working paper / arXiv preprint. That single asset is worth more than 6 months of cold outbound.
